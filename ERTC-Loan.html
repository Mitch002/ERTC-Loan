Employee Retention Tax Credit (ERTC) Loan - Supporting Your Business

Learn How the ERTC Loan Can Benefit Your Company

Introduction to ERTC Loan

The ERTC Loan is a special loan program that allows eligible businesses to receive a tax credit for retaining their employees during the COVID-19 pandemic. The ERTC Loan can help businesses reduce their payroll tax liability, improve their cash flow, and retain their workforce.

Eligibility Criteria

To qualify for the ERTC Loan, businesses must meet the following criteria:

  • They must have been in operation in 2020 and 2021
  • They must have experienced a significant decline in gross receipts or a full or partial suspension of operations due to a government order related to COVID-19
  • They must have paid wages and health benefits to their employees during the eligible quarters Some examples of businesses that qualify for the ERTC Loan are:
  • Restaurants, bars, and cafes that had to limit their capacity or close their indoor dining due to COVID-19 restrictions
  • Retail stores, salons, and gyms that had to reduce their hours or shut down temporarily due to COVID-19 lockdowns
  • Manufacturing, construction, and transportation companies that faced supply chain disruptions or reduced demand due to COVID-19 impacts

Benefits of the ERTC Loan

The ERTC Loan can provide many benefits and advantages for businesses, such as:

  • A tax credit of up to 70% of the qualified wages paid to each employee, up to $10,000 per employee per quarter, for a maximum of $28,000 per employee for 2021
  • A refundable credit that can be claimed in advance or against the employer's share of social security taxes
  • A flexible credit that can be used for any business purpose, such as paying rent, utilities, inventory, or debt
  • A retroactive credit that can be claimed for wages paid in 2020, if the business did not receive a Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) loan or if the PPP loan was not forgiven Here are some testimonials from businesses that have benefited from the ERTC Loan:

How to Apply for the ERTC Loan

The application process for the ERTC Loan is simple and straightforward. Here are the steps to follow:

  • Determine your eligibility for the ERTC Loan by comparing your gross receipts in 2020 and 2021 with the same quarters in 2019, or by identifying the periods when your operations were fully or partially suspended due to a government order related to COVID-19
  • Calculate the amount of qualified wages and health benefits that you paid to your employees during the eligible quarters, excluding any wages that were used to claim the PPP loan or other tax credits
  • Fill out Form 7200, Advance Payment of Employer Credits Due to COVID-19, and submit it to the IRS to request an advance payment of the ERTC Loan, or claim the credit on your quarterly employment tax return, Form 941, Employer's Quarterly Federal Tax Return
  • Keep records of your gross receipts, wages, and health benefits for at least four years, and be prepared to provide them to the IRS upon request

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Here are some common questions and answers about the ERTC Loan:

  • Q: Can I claim the ERTC Loan if I received a PPP loan?
  • A: Yes, you can claim the ERTC Loan if you received a PPP loan, as long as you do not use the same wages to claim both the PPP loan forgiveness and the ERTC Loan. You can also claim the ERTC Loan for wages paid in 2020, if you did not receive a PPP loan or if your PPP loan was not forgiven.
  • Q: How long is the ERTC Loan available?
  • A: The ERTC Loan is available for wages paid from March 13, 2020 to December 31, 2021. However, the credit rate and the wage limit vary depending on the quarter. For 2020, the credit rate is 50% and the wage limit is $10,000 per employee for the entire year. For 2021, the credit rate is 70% and the wage limit is $10,000 per employee per quarter.
  • Q: How do I report the ERTC Loan on my tax return?
  • A: You can report the ERTC Loan on your quarterly employment tax return, Form 941, Employer's Quarterly Federal Tax Return, by reducing your employer's share of social security taxes by the amount of the credit. You can also request an advance payment of the credit by filing Form 7200, Advance Payment of Employer Credits Due to COVID-19, with the IRS.

ERTC Loan Calculation Examples

Here are some examples showing how the ERTC Loan amount is calculated for different scenarios:

  • Example 1: A restaurant with 10 employees had gross receipts of $200,000 in the first quarter of 2019, $150,000 in the first quarter of 2020, and $100,000 in the first quarter of 2021. The restaurant paid $50,000 in qualified wages and health benefits to its employees in the first quarter of 2021. The restaurant is eligible for the ERTC Loan because its gross receipts in the first quarter of 2021 were less than 80% of its gross receipts in the first quarter of 2019. The ERTC Loan amount for the first quarter of 2021 is $35,000, which is 70% of $50,000.
  • Example 2: A manufacturing company with 50 employees had gross receipts of $500,000 in the fourth quarter of 2019, $400,000 in the fourth quarter of 2020, and $300,000 in the fourth quarter of 2021. The company paid $200,000 in qualified wages and health benefits to its employees in the fourth quarter of 2021. The company is eligible for the ERTC Loan because its gross receipts in the fourth quarter of 2021 were less than 80% of its gross receipts in the fourth quarter of 2019. The ERTC Loan amount for the fourth quarter of 2021 is $350,000, which is 70% of $200,000. However, since the company has more than 500 employees, the ERTC Loan is only available for the wages and health benefits paid to the employees who did not work during the quarter.
  • Example 3: A retail store with 5 employees had gross receipts of $100,000 in the second quarter of 2019, $80,000 in the second quarter of 2020, and $90,000 in the second quarter of 2021. The store paid $25,000 in qualified wages and health benefits to its employees in the second quarter of 2021. The store is not eligible for the ERTC Loan because its gross receipts in the second quarter of 2021 were more than 80% of its gross receipts in the second quarter of 2019. The ERTC Loan amount for the second quarter of 2021 is $0.

Legal and Compliance Information

The ERTC Loan is subject to the following legal and compliance information:

  • The ERTC Loan is a tax credit and not a grant or a loan. The ERTC Loan does not need to be repaid, unless it is determined that the business was not eligible for the credit or that the credit was claimed in error.
  • The ERTC Loan is subject to audit and verification by the IRS. The business must keep records of its gross receipts, wages, and health benefits for at least four years, and provide them to the IRS upon request. The business must also comply with the IRS guidance and regulations regarding the ERTC Loan.
  • The ERTC Loan is subject to the limitations and restrictions of the Internal Revenue Code and the Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security (CARES) Act. The ERTC Loan cannot be claimed for wages that were used to claim the PPP loan forgiveness or other tax credits. The ERTC Loan cannot exceed the employer's share of social security taxes for the quarter. The ERTC Loan cannot be claimed for wages paid to certain related individuals or owners of the business.
